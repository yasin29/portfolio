## At a glance

A two-sided marketplace where collectors get their art appraised, bought, or commissioned by vetted experts. I joined as the project manager owning end-to-end delivery — three distinct service flows and a payment architecture spanning Stripe Connect and PayPal authorization holds.

## The problem

Art appraisal is gated by who-you-know. Collectors with one piece can't access the experts who price million-dollar lots, and most appraisal happens through opaque, fee-heavy intermediaries. Artlive's bet: turn appraisal, buyer support, and bespoke commission into productized services with transparent pricing and a platform that handles escrow, refunds, and milestone-based payouts.

## My role

- **Product lead** for the platform: PRD ownership, sprint planning, and backlog grooming across the full platform scope.
- **Liaison** between the Korean client team, a Bangladesh-based engineering team, and external payment providers (Stripe, PayPal).
- **Payment-flow architect**: defined authorization-hold logic, refund decision rules, and the 8% platform fee model in writing before a single line of Stripe code went in.

## Approach

- Split the surface area into **three service flows** — Online Valuation, Buyer Support, Bespoke commission — and ran each as a parallel epic so we could ship the simplest (Online Valuation) first and learn from real expert behaviour before unlocking the more complex Bespoke path.
- Modelled the state machine for orders explicitly: payment authorized → expert assigned → review submitted → release / refund. Every button in the dashboard maps to a single transition, which made QA scope brutally honest.
- Stood up a category taxonomy and onboarding journey for both expert and collector personas so the matching algorithm had clean inputs from day one.
- Built an admin console mirror of every collector + expert action so support could resolve disputes without engineering involvement.

## What shipped

- Collector + Expert onboarding (KYC tier, category preferences, portfolio review)
- Online Valuation flow with structured review form
- Buyer Support flow (pre-purchase opinion service)
- Bespoke commission flow with quote + milestone payments
- Stripe Connect payouts (expert side) + PayPal fallback
- Refund flows: full pre-authorization, partial milestone, dispute-driven
- Admin dashboard: users, orders, activities, payment, automation, calendar, and access settings

## Outcome

- Platform live to expert and collector beta.
- 8% platform fee model validated against expert price elasticity.
- Refund volume kept low by routing high-risk orders through Buyer Support before Bespoke commitment — a flow I lobbied for during PRD review.

## What I'd do differently

Front-load the **dispute** path. We modelled the happy refund flow well, but contested refunds (collector unhappy with appraisal) needed a tighter SLA than we baked in. A formal arbitration tier would have saved 2 sprints of patching.
