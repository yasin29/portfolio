## At a glance

QA on a 3D virtual-tour platform for real-estate listings — the work that taught me how shipping product looks under real production volume. Bridge role from front-end dev into project management.

## The problem

A 3D tour pipeline is unforgiving: one bad stitch, one mis-aligned floor plan, and the listing looks unprofessional. Volume meant manual review at scale couldn't keep up — needed structured test cases and nightly regression on the rendering pipeline.

## My role

- **QC specialist** — authored test cases, ran regression suites, triaged defects with the US product team.
- Worked across the US ↔ Bangladesh timezone gap; the async operating habits transferred directly into PM work after.

## Approach

- Built a **test-case taxonomy** by failure mode (stitching, lighting, alignment, navigation, asset loading) so defect categorisation drove the right fix path.
- Standardised nightly regression on a fixed property set so platform changes had a stable comparison baseline.

## What shipped

- Documented QA process now used as onboarding material for newer QC hires.
- Reduced backlog of unsorted defects to a steady-state queue.

## Outcome

- Personal: the bridge between coding (Care-box) and PM (Kawaii) — a role that taught me to think in product cycles, not just code.

## What I'd do differently

Should have learned **automation** earlier. A lot of the regression we ran by eye could have been screenshot-diffed; I left the role just as I was starting to script things.
