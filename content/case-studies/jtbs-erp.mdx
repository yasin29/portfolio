## At a glance

Customised a cloud ERP (originally a Pakistani retail product, built by **Jin Technologies**) for Bangladeshi garment manufacturers — JB TEX BD and Hameem Group. Inventory, accounting, sales, and HR features re-shaped to match apparel-export workflows.

## The problem

Off-the-shelf ERP rarely fits a vertical. Apparel manufacturers track work-in-progress across cutting, sewing, finishing, and shipping; their costing model uses style/colour/size combos; their invoicing has to reconcile against international LCs. JTBS as shipped covered ~70% of this generically — the remaining 30% was the difference between "we use it" and "it sits unused."

## My role

- **PM** between the Japan-based product team, the Pakistan-based core engineering team, and the Bangladeshi client floors.
- **Requirements translator** — walked factory floors to map the actual workflow (not the org chart), wrote SRS, owned UAT scripts.
- **Cross-border delivery** — Bangladesh ↔ Japan ↔ Pakistan timezone handoff; sprint cadence had to survive three holidays calendars.

## Approach

- Started with **two factory walk-throughs** before writing any spec. Maps drawn by hand, then digitised.
- Split customisations into **must-have** (apparel WIP tracking, multi-warehouse with bonded warehouse logic, FBR-equivalent local tax for BD) and **nice-to-have** — and rigidly defended the line.
- Ran UAT with operators, not managers — managers would say "looks fine"; operators caught the workflow breaks.
- Built an internal "ERP customisation playbook" for the team — every future apparel client now starts from a documented baseline.

## What shipped

- Apparel-specific BOM and WIP modules
- Multi-warehouse inventory with bonded-warehouse separation (export compliance)
- Local tax + invoicing localisation for Bangladesh
- Operator training materials in Bangla
- Reporting suite tied to the manufacturing cadence (daily output, monthly cost)

## Outcome

- Two enterprise clients onboarded (JB TEX BD, Hameem Group — both significant Bangladeshi apparel exporters).
- Internal playbook reused for subsequent ERP engagements.
- Validated Kawaii's "Bangladesh apparel + Japan tech" cross-border model — directly relevant to Findy Job which followed.

## What I'd do differently

Push back harder on **mid-project scope expansion**. Both clients tried to add HR & payroll modules mid-build; we partially agreed and slowed delivery. A clearer contract phase would have parked these as Phase 2.
